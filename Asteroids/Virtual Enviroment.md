  
Virtual [[enviroment]]s are Python's way to keep dependencies (e.g., the `pygame` module) separate from other projects on our machine. For example, we need `pygame` version 2 for this project, but another project on your computer might require version 1.

As a best practice, each Python [[project]] on your machine should have its own virtual environment to keep them isolated from each other.
